Forget templates. Pick an experience.

These are concepts, not colour schemes. How do you want someone to FEEL when they visit?

A

Cinematic Reveal

Opens like a film. Black screen, your name fades in letter by letter. As you scroll, sections appear like scenes — dramatic, slow, intentional. Big full-screen moments. Text and image reveals timed like a trailer. Feels like watching something, not browsing a website.

Inspo: film title sequences, Apple product pages, Awwwards winners

B

Interactive Chaos (Ordered)

Looks like Amanda's desk or brain — sticky note fun facts, a music player, a book stack, scattered elements you can click and pick up. But underneath the chaos is a clean structure. Hovering things triggers reactions. Feels like exploring a room, not reading a CV.

Inspo: bryantcodes.art physics, creative portfolio experiments

C

Bold Brutalist

Rules? None. Giant raw typography that breaks the grid. Text overlaps images. Section numbers in huge contrast. Asymmetric layout that makes you stop and look twice. Black, white, one punchy accent. Feels like a zine or a protest poster — confident, intentional rule-breaking.

Inspo: wodniack.dev boldness, editorial brutalism

D

Dark Immersive Story

Very dark base. As you scroll, content lights up like a spotlight — the rest stays dark. Your story is told section by section like chapters. Cursor leaves a soft glow trail. Feels intimate and personal, like someone reading you their journal in a dark room.

Inspo: editorial dark mode, immersive long-form storytelling sites
